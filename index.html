Music
Colonel Macabre / Pacing / Void Participant / Buddy JuniorJul 8
Jade Cathay Chinese Restaurant, San Jose · 7pm · all ages · $10–15, no one turned away

Your first night. Four local Bay Area indie bands playing a mix of dreamy shoegaze (think hazy, effects-heavy guitars layered over vocals) and folksy indie rock. The venue is a Chinese restaurant — dim sum included — so you eat, hang out, and the bands play around you. It's a small, community DIY show, not a big concert hall. The kind of show where you're 10 feet from the band.
Schlong / Party Force / Bobby Joe Ebola / Flamango BayJul 10
924 Gilman Street, Berkeley · 7pm · all ages · $15

924 Gilman is a legendary DIY punk venue that Green Day and Rancid came out of in the early 90s. It's volunteer-run, all ages, completely sober (no alcohol, no drugs), and has been going since 1985. The bands on this bill are punk and hardcore — fast, loud, unpolished in the best way. Bobby Joe Ebola has been a Bay Area punk fixture for 30 years. This is a genuine piece of punk history as a venue, not just a show.
Have a Good Season / Fieldrush / Apperson / Star 99 / BlazerJul 12
Black & Brown, San Jose · 7pm doors, 7:30pm music · all ages · $15

Put on by Heavy Lemon, the crew behind most of San Jose's best DIY shows. Have a Good Season is a touring indie/emo band from New Jersey, Fieldrush is indie rock from San Diego — both on the road specifically for this show. The local San Jose bands open. This is the kind of show that feels like being in on something: small room, people who actually care about the music, bands that are genuinely good but not famous yet.
